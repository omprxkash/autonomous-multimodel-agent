# DueCrew — Go-To-Market Plan (first 90 days)

## Positioning

"Am I good to work?" — one screen, red/amber/green, for every card, cert, and certificate
your trade requires. Not a suite. Not for the GC. For you.

## Channel 1 (primary): CE course providers — partner, don't fight

The continuing-education ecosystem ($9–10 online courses, e.g. expertce.com,
hlonlinece.com, anytimecertification.com — live sellers verified by search 2026-07-12) has
exactly our audience at exactly the moment they think about renewal — then loses them for a
year. Pitch to providers: DueCrew reminds their students *through the year* and routes them
back for next cycle's hours (affiliate link inside the CE ledger). Their email list, our
retention loop. Target: 3 provider partnerships in 90 days. This channel is why the CE
ledger links to "approved providers" rather than trying to sell courses ourselves.

## Channel 2: The "Lapse Math" one-pager (top-of-funnel asset — built, see extras/)

A printable worksheet that lets a tradesperson compute their own cost-of-a-lapse number
(renewal fee vs delinquent fee vs days-off-job × day rate). Distributed as a PDF-style
print page in trade Facebook groups, forums, and supply-house counters. It's useful with or
without DueCrew — which is why it will actually get shared. QR/URL footer points to the
site's waitlist.

## Channel 3: State-page SEO (compounding)

150 evergreen pages: {state} × {electrician, plumber, HVAC} — "When does my Texas
journeyman electrician license expire? What CE do I need?" Each page is a genuine answer
(from the state-rules dataset) with the product underneath. These queries have clear intent,
low competition outside CE-course sellers, and refresh annually. The dataset that powers the
product powers the funnel — one maintenance cost, two outputs.

## Channel 4: Trade forums & YouTube trades creators

Honest founder posts in the paperwork/administration threads where the pain already lives
(ElectricianTalk, ContractorTalk, HVAC-Talk, r/electricians) — show the board, ask for
brutal feedback, founding-member offer. Sponsor 2–3 mid-size trades YouTubers (integration
read: "what happens if your ticket lapses" story format). Draft posts in
extras/launch-posts.md.

## Launch sequence

- **Weeks 1–2:** waitlist site + Lapse Math sheet + 10-state rules dataset live. Founding
  member offer opens (cap 200).
- **Weeks 3–6:** forum/group posts, first CE-provider outreach, state pages publishing
  10/week.
- **Weeks 7–12:** onboarding-email sequence live (extras/onboarding-emails.md), first
  YouTube integration, iterate on activation (goal: credential board filled in < 10 minutes
  — with a "text us a photo of your license and we'll set it up" concierge onboarding).

## Activation & retention mechanics

- Concierge onboarding (photo → we type it in) — removes the one real adoption barrier.
- COI share link = weekly utility; CE ledger = monthly; alerts = the annual save.
- Renewal-day moment: "You're good through 2028" green banner — screenshot-friendly,
  designed to get posted in the same forums.

## What we will NOT do at launch

Paid search (CE sellers own the auction), app-store apps (PWA first), GC-side sales
(wrong side of the table — see thesis), and any state where our rules data isn't verified.
