DueCrew — run-2 recap

A compliance deadline guard for solo licensed trade contractors: license renewals, CE hours, insurance and bond dates on one red/amber/green board, with alerts that get louder. Built end-to-end from open-internet research in one autonomous run — nothing published, nothing purchased, every claim traced to a fetched URL or flagged.

The business in five minutes

Who: the ~1.76M electricians, plumbers and HVAC techs (BLS May 2025) — especially the solo operators among America's ~2.88M no-employee construction businesses — whose right to earn is gated by a stack of expiring documents and whose "system" is a glovebox and memory.

"Any work performed while the license is expired is considered to be unlicensed and disciplinary action can be taken against you." California Contractors State License Board — fetched primary source; the pain is regulatory, not cosmetic.

What: one screen answering "Am I good to work?" — every credential as a Good/Watch/Act card; escalating SMS/email alerts (90→1 days); a CE ledger that counts hours against what the state actually requires (Texas verified against TDLR in v0.1); a COI locker with a live share link for GCs; and a per-state rules library where every row shows its source and verification status.

Why it wins the niche: generic expiry-reminder tools know nothing about trades; every funded compliance player (SkillSignal, SimpleCerts, PermitFlow's $54M GC-side world) sells to the GC to police subs. Nobody found in this run's scans builds for the tradesperson's side of the table. Model: $12/mo · $99/yr solo, $29/mo crew, 200 founding lifetime seats at $199 as the built-in willingness-to-pay test.

Why you might not be convinced — the red team's surviving objections

Willingness to pay is unproven. No customer could be contacted in this run. The $199 founding-seat sale is the designed falsification test: if 200 seats don't sell in ~90 days, the thesis fails cheaply.
The moat is speed, not structure. The alert primitive is a weekend build; four generic expiry tools already exist. The bet is the maintained, cited state-rules dataset + trade-native workflows — copyable in months by anyone funded.
Annual value events invite seasonal churn. COI share link and CE ledger are the retention mitigations; they are plausible, not proven.
The $47,000 anchor is a vendor anecdote — real, fetched, but an insurance agency's client story, labeled as such everywhere it appears.
