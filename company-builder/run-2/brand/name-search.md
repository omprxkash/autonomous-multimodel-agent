200 = registered) for .com; rdap.org bootstrap for other TLDs; WebSearch for trademark-ish
collisions. **Nothing was purchased** (guardrail: check availability, don't buy).

## Chosen: **DueCrew**

- **duecrew.com — unregistered** (Verisign RDAP returned 404, checked 2026-07-12). Not purchased.
- WebSearch for `"DueCrew"` found no company, product, or app of that name (results were
  Crew (crewapp.com), OneCrew (paving FSM), Due.com (invoicing) — all distinct names/marks).
- Note for a real founder: this is a knockout search, not a trademark clearance. A USPTO
  TESS search and counsel review are required before spending on the mark. (Disclosed in
  honesty.md.)

## Why the name works

- "Due" = the entire product in one word; "Crew" = who it's for, and the crew plan.
- Says what it does at a glance, rhymes, two syllables each, spells itself over the phone.
- Reads right on a van door, a hex-badge app icon, and a forum signature.
- Brand line falls out naturally: **"DueCrew watches the dates so you can work."**
