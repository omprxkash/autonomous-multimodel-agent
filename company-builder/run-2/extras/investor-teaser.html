DueCrew

Compliance deadline guard for America's solo licensed trades — license renewals, CE hours, insurance and bond dates on one red/amber/green board.

Pre-launch · seeking feedback, not funds
duecrew.com (unregistered, checked 2026-07-12)

The problem, in the regulator's words

"Any work performed while the license is expired is considered to be unlicensed and disciplinary action can be taken against you" — CSLB. One documented COI lapse: 11 days off the job, $47,000 withheld (Texas agency client account). The people most exposed — solo electricians, plumbers, HVAC techs — have no office manager and reject field-service suites as overbuilt. Their system today is a glovebox and memory.

$54MPermitFlow Series B (Dec 2025) — compliance-adjacent heat, GC-side
~$99MSAM at $99/yr × ~1M solo licensees (inference, stated assumptions)

Product

  • Status board: every credential, days-left, Good/Watch/Act
  • Escalating SMS/email alerts, 90 days → day-before
  • CE ledger vs. actual state requirements (TX verified v0.1)
  • COI locker + live share link (GC-facing viral loop)
  • Registry monitoring beta — verification, not just reminders

Wedge

Every funded player in contractor compliance sells to the GC to police subs. DueCrew is the sub's side of the table. The maintained per-state per-trade rules dataset compounds; it powers both product and 150-page SEO funnel.

Model

  • Solo $12/mo · $99/yr; Crew $29/mo; founding lifetime $199 × 200
  • >90% gross margin; SMS + rules-maintenance are the only real COGS
  • GTM: CE-provider partnerships, printable "Lapse Math" lead magnet, state-page SEO, trade forums

Falsification test

If 200 founding seats don't sell to real tradespeople in ~90 days, the thesis fails. That test costs almost nothing to run.

What the red team says (we wrote it down)

  • Moat is speed, not structure — the reminder primitive is a weekend build; the dataset is months to copy (R1/R2).
  • WTP unproven — no customer interviews were possible in this exercise; founding-seat sales are the designed test (R3).
  • Annual value events risk seasonal churn — weekly COI utility and monthly CE touch are mitigations, unproven (R4).
  • Rules-data liability — a stale rule is a real failure mode; every row ships with source + last-verified date (R5).
  • $47k anchor is a vendor anecdote — real page, labeled as agency client account; replace with first-party story post-launch (R9).
