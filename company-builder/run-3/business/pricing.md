## WideTally pricing

### Free — WideTally Lite
Full features, max 2 books, forever. Not time-boxed.
*Why:* the 44% who earn ≤$100/mo become users, evangelists, and eventually 3+-book customers; a time-boxed trial would burn them. Matches the community's allergy to rent-seeking.

### $59 one-time — WideTally License
Unlimited books/pen names, all parsers, 12 months of format updates included.
*Why $59:* under one year of any incumbent subscription (cheapest real competitor ≈ $120/yr); above impulse-tier so it signals a maintained tool, not abandonware; TrackerBox precedent proves pay-once acceptance. Round comparison for marketing: "costs less than four months of the tool that keeps logging you out."

### $19/yr — Format Updates Pass (after year 1, optional)
The app never breaks retroactively: formats it already parses keep working offline forever. The pass buys new/changed retailer formats.
*Why:* this is the honest version of a subscription — it prices exactly the part of the product that has recurring cost (parser maintenance against format drift), and nothing else. Sublime Text/JetBrains-style perpetual-fallback is a proven model with developer-adjacent audiences.

## Revenue model honesty (ESTIMATES, all labeled)
- Year-1 SOM target: 1,000 licenses = $59,000 + Lite funnel. Assumes: Kindlepreneur review or listing (their roundup is the category kingmaker), 2–3 community launches, registry SEO. This is an assumption about channel performance, not a verified fact.
- Steady state (ESTIMATE): licenses decay without marketing; updates-pass attach modeled at 40–60% of active users (JetBrains-style renewal rates are public precedent, not fetched this run — INFERENCE).
- Failure mode priced in: even at 200 licenses ($11.8k), founder cost is one person's part-time maintenance; the business fails soft, not hard.
