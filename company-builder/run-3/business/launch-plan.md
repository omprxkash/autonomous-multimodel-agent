# WideTally — 30-Day Launch Plan
2026-07-13. Channels chosen because the verified complaints live there (see research/). Nothing in this plan was executed — guardrail: publish nothing. This is the playbook a founder runs.

## Positioning line
**"Every store. One ledger. Your machine."** — WideTally reads the royalty reports you already download and shows what each book actually earns after ads. Nothing uploads. Ever.

## Week 0 (prep, days 1–7)
1. Register widetally.com (checked available 2026-07-13 via RDAP; not purchased in this run).
2. Ship the Lite build + sample-data playground (the `product/` demo is exactly this) so every link lands on something that works in 10 seconds without a signup.
3. Publish the **Report Format Registry** (`business/format-registry.md`) as free documentation — the linkable asset that makes WideTally the reference for "what's in a KDP royalty report."
4. Prepare honest comparison page (vs ScribeCount/Publisher Champ/Book Report) using only verifiable public pricing/facts — the fetched sources in this run are the citation set.

## Week 1 — the wide-author beachhead (days 8–14)
- **r/selfpublish** (1.1M+ members; the verified pain threads are here): launch post in the community's native format — "I built a local tracker because I was tired of X" show-and-tell with the free playground link. Rules-compliant self-promo; engage every comment for 72h.
- **Wide for the Win** (the Facebook group *named after* the wide strategy) and 20BooksTo50K-successor communities: same show-don't-tell post adapted; these groups ban hard selling — lead with the free registry documentation.
- **Kindlepreneur outreach**: Dave Chesson's roundup is the category kingmaker (his review withdrew the ScribeCount recommendation — the exact opening). Pitch: review unit + the reliability angle his own review raised. One respectful email, one follow-up, never more.

## Week 2 — the trust story (days 15–21)
- Guest posts/podcast pitches to author-business shows (Six Figure Authors-style, The Creative Penn-tier as reach goal) with the one story angle that's different: **"your income data shouldn't live on someone else's server."**
- Publish "How I verify WideTally never phones home" — a technical post inviting readers to check with devtools/firewall; converts the audience's skepticism into the moat.
- ProductHunt-style launch deliberately **skipped**: the buyer isn't there. (Logged as a decision; most launches waste week 2 on PH vanity.)

## Week 3 — the ads-ROI wedge (days 22–28)
- Content: "Your ACOS is lying to you" — royalty-based ad math explained (the demo's Ads view is the illustration). This is the sharpest verified pain with money attached ($81–$4,500/mo spend, WWM survey).
- Written Word Media / ALLi (Alliance of Independent Authors) partner outreach: both run tool roundups and have surveyed exactly this audience.

## Week 4 — close the loop (days 29–30)
- Ship one visible parser update from a user report ("Kobo changed a column — fixed in 36 hours") and publicize it in-community: demonstrates the updates-pass value prop live.
- Collect the first 10 testimonial permissions from Lite users.

## Budget
$0 ad spend for month one by design (the ICP distrusts ads and lives in communities where ads don't reach). Costs: domain (~$12), code-signing cert (~$100/yr, needed for a local app to be trusted on Windows/macOS — ESTIMATE), review units (free licenses).

## Success metrics (30 days)
- 1,000 playground sessions; 300 Lite installs; 50 licenses ($2,950); 1 kingmaker conversation started (Kindlepreneur/ALLi/WWM); registry ranking for 3 long-tail "royalty report" queries.
- Kill signal honestly stated: if Lite→license conversion <2% after 500 installs, pricing or product is wrong — revisit before spending on growth.
