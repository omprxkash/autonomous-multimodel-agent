## Rubric (100 pts)
- **Pain severity × frequency (25)** — how much it hurts, how often, verified.
- **Willingness to pay (20)** — proven spend in the exact category.
- **Open flank / differentiation (20)** — is there a defensible, underserved wedge vs incumbents?
- **Demo honesty (15)** — can a local static+Node demo honestly represent the real product?
- **Reachable GTM (10)** — can a solo founder reach buyers this month without ad spend?
- **Series differentiation (10)** — distance from run-1 (Shopify B2B SaaS) and run-2 (B2B/SMB).

## Key judgments (why the scores fell this way)
- **#5 inspectors lost on the flank + GTM despite the highest raw pain.** The rescue market is already crowded (Tap Inspect, Inspector Toolbelt, Inspect Forge, HomeGauge — all recommended inside the verified threads), the "trust" wedge is copyable marketing rather than architecture, and the community's moderator is actively banning software startups for farming the Spectora backlash (verified 32-pt mod post) — the exact channel a new entrant needs is hostile. Real product also lives on mobile/on-site, which a browser demo under-represents (demo honesty).
- **#1 won on architecture-as-differentiation.** KDP has no public API, so every incumbent runs on browser extensions or uploads of the same report files the author already downloads. A local-first tool that reads those files is the *same data path with strictly less risk* (no credentials shared, no downtime, works offline) — and the market leader for wide authors is verifiably failing on exactly reliability ("tried to open the program to see my data and got nothing" — Kindlepreneur). Local-first here is a structural wedge, not a slogan, and it makes the run's demo *be* the product rather than imitate it.
- **#2 podcast packaging** is real and chronic but the wedge is "cheaper/consolidated AI," which is a knife-fight against fast-moving funded incumbents (Descript).
- **#6 homelab** died on WTP as predicted ("Nobody cares that much lol" — 99pts).

## Winner
**Candidate 1: local-first royalty + ad-ROI dashboard for self-published authors.** Runner-up (promoted if the skeptic pass breaks the winner): #5 inspector software.
