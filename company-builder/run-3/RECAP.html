WideTally Autonomous build · Run 3 · July 2026

WideTally is a local-first royalty & ad-ROI ledger for self-published authors who sell beyond Amazon. It reads the report files authors already download — on their machine, never a server — and shows what every book actually earns after ads. $59 once, against incumbents at $120–$348/yr.

Fictional company, built end-to-end as a research exercise. Every external claim links to a source fetched during the run. Voiceover is Windows TTS; sample data is synthetic — both disclosed on-screen. Full disclosures: honesty.md.

The thesis in 60 seconds

  1. Verified pain: wide authors reconcile money across 3–8 dashboards with no API and no common format — a daily/monthly ritual they hate.
  2. Verified vacuum: the leading wide-author tracker is publicly called unreliable by the category's kingmaker reviewer; the only local-first predecessor (TrackerBox) was abandoned in 2019; Amazon confirmed in writing there is no API — so every tool ultimately lives off the same downloadable files.
  3. The move: parse those files locally. Same data path as the cloud tools, minus the credentials, downtime, and subscription. The demo below is the architecture, not a mock of it.
“On average it takes me 15 minutes to adequately review all sales platforms… that would equate to 92 hours a year wasted.”Dave Chesson, Kindlepreneur — source (fetched)

What a skeptic should read first

The red team ran after the build and drew blood. Highlights (full report: red-team.md):

  • WOUNDWideTally serves monthly reconciliation and ad decisions — it cannot serve the compulsive daily-refresh moment the pain evidence also contains. One landing-page line still overclaims this; left visible deliberately.
  • WOUNDThe “leader is failing” claim rests heavily on one (authoritative, affiliate-funded) reviewer, and may have a shelf life.
  • WOUNDPrivacy as a purchase driver is an unproven hypothesis — the verified pains are reliability and money. The launch plan carries an explicit kill-signal metric.
  • WOUNDTrackerBox’s 2019 abandonment warns that one-time pricing may underfund the parser treadmill; the Updates Pass is the bet that it won’t.
