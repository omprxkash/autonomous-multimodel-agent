How it works

There is no Amazon API for author royalties — Amazon has said so themselves. Every tracker ultimately lives off the report files you can already download. WideTally just skips the part where your income data visits someone else’s server.

Download your reports

The same KDP, Draft2Digital, Kobo and Amazon Ads files you already export. You keep doing what you do — no new logins, no browser extension watching your dashboard.

Drop them on WideTally

Format detection is automatic, against a registry we publish openly. Parsing happens on your machine — flight-mode friendly.

Read your one ledger

Per-book, per-series, per-store earnings. KU pages priced at the month’s rate. Ad spend set against the royalties it actually bought.

How this works

Your income is nobody’s dataset

WideTally never asks for your KDP password, never reads your browser session, never sees a number. Verify it yourself: open your system’s network monitor while you work — there’s nothing to find. The parsers will be published source-available, and releases are checksummed. Privacy claims you can test beat privacy claims you have to trust.

Questions authors actually ask

Why not just connect to my accounts like other tools?

Because there’s nothing to connect to. Amazon confirmed in writing (Oct 2025) that no API returns KDP data. Tools that “connect” are scraping dashboards or riding a browser extension on your logged-in session — which is why they break, log you out, and ask you to re-authenticate. The report files are the ground truth. You already have them.

Is my Kindle Unlimited income exact?

No — and it isn’t exact in any tool, because Amazon announces the per-page rate after the month ends. WideTally prices pages at the published rate once it’s out and labels everything before that “est.” We’d rather show you a labeled estimate than an unlabeled guess.

What happens when a store changes its report format?

Our format registry — which we publish openly — gets updated, usually within days, and your app picks up the new parser with the Updates Pass. Formats your app already knows are parsed locally forever, pass or no pass. This is the actual hard part of this product; we priced it honestly instead of hiding it in a subscription.

Which platforms at launch?

Amazon KDP (royalties, orders, KENP), Amazon Ads campaign exports, Draft2Digital, and Kobo Writing Life. Next on the registry: IngramSpark, ACX/Audible, Google Play, Apple. If you can export it, we can learn to read it.

Mac? Windows? Phone?

WideTally runs anywhere a modern browser runs, as a local app — including fully offline once installed. Your ledger is a folder of files you can back up, sync, or move like anything else you own.

Is this a subscription in disguise?

No. $59 buys the app outright. The $19/yr pass only buys future format changes. If you never renew, everything you bought keeps working on every file format it shipped with. Compare: three years of the cheapest cloud tracker costs more than five times as much.
