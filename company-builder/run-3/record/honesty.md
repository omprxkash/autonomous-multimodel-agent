## The company itself
- **WideTally is fictional.** No entity exists, nothing was published, no domain was purchased (widetally.com availability was *checked* via Verisign RDAP on 2026-07-13 — HTTP 404 = unregistered at that moment; that can change any time).
- No customers, no testimonials. The three quotes on the landing page are **public statements about the problem space by independent people**, labeled as such on the page, each linking to its source. They are not endorsements of WideTally.
- Revenue figures (1,000 licenses year-1, updates-pass attach rates) are **founder estimates, labeled ESTIMATE/INFERENCE where they appear**. No market test occurred.

## The product demo
- All sample data is **synthetic** (fictional author "R. E. Halloran"), generated by `_tools/gen-sample-data.js` with a seeded PRNG, and labeled synthetic in the app banner, footer, sample folder README, and videos.
- Sample file **layouts are modeled on published schemas, not real exports**: the KDP columns match Amazon's own term definitions (fetched — see `business/format-registry.md` ✅ items); the D2D/Kobo/Amazon-Ads layouts are practitioner-knowledge reconstructions (registry ◐ items) and may deviate from real exports. The parsers are therefore proven against our own synthetic formats only. Real KDP workbooks are XLSX; the demo parses CSV.
- KENP per-page rates in the demo (~$0.0044–0.0046) are **plausible synthetic values**, not fetched official monthly rates. (Real anchor verified: Amazon's own footer states the May 2026 KDP Select fund was $70.3M.)
- The demo genuinely parses dropped CSV files locally in the browser — that claim is real and testable. The shipped-product gap (installers, code signing, XLSX, PWA/Tauri packaging) is **not** built; see red-team item 8.
