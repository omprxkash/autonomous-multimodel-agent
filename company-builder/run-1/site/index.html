Four checks between a sync and your live inventory

Every sync passes through the same pipeline: preview, validate, commit, reversible. No step is skippable in the wrong direction. The diff table is the easy part — the circuit breaker and the journaled, reconcilable rollback are the system underneath it.

  1. 1

    Dry-run

    Run the whole sync against live data with writes disabled. You get the complete change set and a report — and your stores don't feel a thing.

  2. 2

    Diff preview

    A human-readable table of exactly what will change: SKU, store, old quantity → new quantity, and where the change came from. You approve before anything is written.

  3. 3

    Circuit breaker

    Rules watch every batch for destructive patterns — "about to zero 2,000 SKUs," "catalog value dropping 90%" — and halt the sync before a single write. Held, not applied.

  4. 4

    Rollback

    Every applied batch is journaled with prior values. If anything is ever wrong, one click restores every quantity to its pre-sync state — with the audit log to prove it.

Questions merchants actually ask

What happens when a sync fails?

Nothing — to your inventory. ReconStock stages every change before writing, so a failure mid-sync leaves your live quantities untouched. If a batch is partially applied when an error occurs, the journal already holds every prior value, and the batch is either completed with compare-and-set checks or rolled back to its pre-sync state. You get a plain-language report: what was attempted, what was written, what was held, and why.

Can ReconStock itself zero out my inventory?

We won't claim "never" — no software vendor honestly can, and a burned merchant shouldn't believe one who does. What we promise is that ReconStock fails safe and is fully reversible: it installs read-only (dry-run only) until you grant write access; writes use Shopify's compare-and-set (compareQuantity) so we never clobber a quantity that changed under us; the circuit breaker halts mass-destructive batches before any write; destructive batches are never auto-applied; and every applied batch is journaled and reversible in one click.

Do I have to approve every sync by hand?

At install, ReconStock is read-only: it dry-runs and shows diffs but cannot write anything until you grant write access — you watch the safety net work before you trust it. After that: only at first, and only if you want to. Standard runs semi-automatic: routine changes apply after review rules pass, and anything unusual waits for you. Pro adds scheduled auto-sync with the circuit breaker armed — the guardrails stay on even when you're not watching.

Does this replace my current sync app?

For store-to-store inventory quantities, yes — that's exactly the surface v1 covers, deliberately narrow because it's the highest-damage one. Product details, prices, and multi-channel connectors (Amazon, eBay, Etsy, TikTok Shop) are on the roadmap. You can also run the Free tier alongside an incumbent to dry-run and audit what it's doing before you switch.

How does billing work?

Through Shopify's own billing, on your existing Shopify invoice — no separate card on file. Upgrade, downgrade, or cancel any time; plan limits are by SKU count and store count, listed above.
