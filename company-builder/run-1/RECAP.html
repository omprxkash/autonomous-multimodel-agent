ReconStock

The only Shopify inventory sync that shows every change before it writes — and lets you undo it.

This page is the complete recap of an autonomous company-building run (2026-07-11): from open-internet pain research to a verified product demo, brand, business plan, videos, and a red-team stress test. Every artifact is linked below. Nothing was published; nothing was purchased.

The business in one minute

Multi-store Shopify merchants pay $10–60/mo for inventory-sync apps that occasionally fail catastrophically — zeroing or negating live inventory across thousands of SKUs (verified reviews as recent as June 2026). Every incumbent syncs first and apologizes later. ReconStock inverts that: read-only dry-run by default, a diff preview of every change before it's written, an anomaly circuit-breaker that halts disasters ("this sync would zero 2,143 SKUs — halted"), and journaled one-click rollback. It doesn't promise to never fail — it promises to fail safe and be fully reversible.

How this run worked

  1. Pain hunt — 8 parallel researchers swept Reddit (via mirror APIs when blocked), Hacker News, G2/Capterra/BBB, vertical pro forums, app marketplaces, Indie Hackers, and SMB communities → 33 raw findings merged into 20 candidates.
  2. Tournament — five judge personas scored all 20 on pain, urgency, reachability, willingness-to-pay, buildability, incumbent weakness → 4 finalists. Full bracket.
  3. Adversarial verification — skeptics re-fetched every finalist's evidence. It worked: one finalist's headline stat was exposed as misattributed, another's willingness-to-pay evidence as synthetic. C09 was the only finalist whose evidence strengthened. Verdicts.
  4. Debate & vote — advocate vs. skeptic memos per finalist, then three fresh judges: 1-1-1 first round, unanimous 3-0 for C09 after deliberation.
  5. Business design — live competitor teardown (all five incumbents' listings/pricing), Shopify API feasibility (inventorySetQuantities compare-and-set, bulk operations), pricing and unit economics. Plan · Market research · Launch plan.
  6. Brand — 20 names screened for trademark + domain collisions → ReconStock (reconstock.com/.io/.app all NXDOMAIN); 6 logo candidates critiqued → "diff brackets" mark. Name · Guidelines · Logo critique.
  7. Build — working product demo + landing page, driven end-to-end by Playwright: 34/34 checks passed. Screenshot-verified on desktop and mobile (390×844, zero overflow). Proof: breaker halt, diff preview, rollback, mobile.
  8. Red team — six skeptic personas, 33 attacks · 0 kills, 19 fixes applied to plan, site, and product (including the messaging rule: never promise "never corrupts"), 14 risks accepted with reasons. Verdict: VIABLE-WITH-FIXES.
  9. Videos — recorded from the real product UI via Playwright, assembled locally with ffmpeg; founder voiceover is Windows TTS (disclosed). Scripts: launch · founder.
